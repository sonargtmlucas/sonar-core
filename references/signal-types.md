# Buying Signals Worth Acting On

The signal matters less than the combination. A single signal is noise. Three signals in the same account in the same week is a buying window.

## The 6 signal categories

### 1. Revenue hiring
SDR, AE, RevOps, Growth, Sales leadership, or BDR roles open.
Why it matters: they're scaling GTM and need infrastructure to match the headcount investment.
Strength: high — active budget, active need, short decision window.

### 2. Leadership change
New CRO, VP Sales, Head of Growth, or founder transitioning from founder-led sales.
Why it matters: new revenue leaders spend in the first 90 days to prove themselves. Old vendors get reviewed. New vendors get shots.
Strength: very high — the "new sheriff" window is 60–90 days.

### 3. First-party activity
Website visits, content engagement, product signups, dormant CRM account reactivation.
Why it matters: they're already in market, already aware, just not ready to raise their hand.
Strength: medium (depends on volume and recency) — highest intent of any signal category.

### 4. Expansion signals
New market entry, product launch, acquisition, new location, or major partnership.
Why it matters: expansion creates new pipeline needs and budget justification.
Strength: medium-high — growth phase companies invest in GTM.

### 5. Technology shift
New CRM adoption, outbound tool migration, RevOps stack changes.
Why it matters: if they're buying HubSpot + Apollo, they're trying to build a system. They'll pay for the intelligence layer.
Strength: high — they're already in buying mode for adjacent tools.

### 6. Public pain
LinkedIn posts complaining about pipeline, job descriptions listing "fix our outbound," community threads asking for tool recommendations, founder posting about hiring SDRs.
Why it matters: they've identified the problem publicly — they're in discovery mode.
Strength: medium — needs fast follow-up before a competitor gets there.

## How to use signals in scoring
See skill: gtm/score-lead.md

ICP fit is table stakes. Signal combination determines timing. A perfect ICP company with zero signals is not the right target this week. A 70% ICP fit company with a new CRO + revenue hiring is a priority now.

## Signal combinations that warrant immediate action
- New CRO hired + SDR hiring = leadership change + revenue scaling → act within 2 weeks
- Funding event + RevOps hiring = growth phase + building GTM → act within 2 weeks
- CRM adoption + public pain = tech in place but stuck → act within 1 week
- First-party activity + leadership change = warm + in-market → act within 3 days

## Where to find signals
- LinkedIn (hiring posts, leadership change announcements): Apify actor apidojo/linkedin-company-scraper
- Job boards (BDR/SDR/RevOps roles): Apify actor
- Crunchbase / news (funding): Apify or direct API
- Technology intelligence: BuiltWith API or Clay enrichment
- First-party (website visits): requires client to install visitor identification (Clearbit, RB2B, or similar)
