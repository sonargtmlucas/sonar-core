# Sonar Narrative — Sales Deck Summary

Source: Drive Docs/Sonar External Sales Deck.pdf

## The problem (slide 2)
Modern GTM is loud. Revenue is quiet.
Teams added tools, data, sequences, and AI — but still struggle to answer the only question that matters: which accounts deserve attention right now?

- Static lists: every account looks equally important until a real buying window is missed
- Tool sprawl: CRM, Apollo, LinkedIn, enrichment, email, and reporting rarely share one brain
- Fake context: "Saw your post" is not relevance. Buyers respond to timing and commercial need.

## The insight (slide 3)
Pipeline starts before the reply.
A prospect usually moves before they respond: hiring, leadership change, expansion, website behavior, product usage, funding, dormant CRM activity, technology shifts, or public pain.

The three questions that matter:
- FIT — is this company worth pursuing?
- MOVEMENT — what changed that makes timing relevant?
- ACTION — who should act, what should they say, and through which channel?

## What Sonar is (slide 4)
A market-sensing system with two modes.
Sonar is the intelligence layer between market movement and revenue action.
It listens for signals, runs directed pulses, and learns from outcomes.

- LISTEN — always-on capture from web, CRM, social, hiring, product, content, and account movement
- INTELLIGENCE CORE — identity, context, scoring, play selection, routing, and learning
- PULSE — directed activation around a vertical, account list, campaign, or hypothesis

## Example workflow (slide 6)
A dormant target account hires a new CRO, visits your pricing page, and starts hiring SDRs.

Signal detected → Context enriched → Account scored → Play selected → Rep routed → Outcome logged

The output is not "send more email." The output is an explainable recommendation: why this account, why now, who to contact, what to say, and whether the action should be automated or human-led.

## Good fit / not a fit (slide 10)
Good fit: B2B SaaS or tech-enabled services, 20–150 employees, $15K+ ACV, existing CRM + outbound stack, inconsistent pipeline, founder or revenue leader wants sharper timing.

Not a fit: no clear ICP, very low ACV, no sales owner, no CRM discipline, wants cheap appointment-setting, believes more email volume fixes everything.

"The first question is not 'how many leads?' It is 'what movements actually indicate buying intent for your specific offer?'"

## How to engage (slide 8)
- Deploy Sonar: for revenue teams that need better timing, prioritization, and action. Always-on listening + directed pulse campaigns + managed operation inside your existing stack.
- Build with Sonar: for high-value workflows that need intelligence and action beyond GTM. Research, proposals, onboarding, customer success, reporting, and operational coordination.

Revenue is the first focus. The system architecture is bigger than lead generation.

## Key differentiators to use in sales conversations
1. Intelligence before outreach — we don't start with a list and ask how many messages to send
2. Buying windows not leads — fit + movement + timing + context + value
3. Explainable action — always: why this account, why now, what happens next
4. No token math — clients buy outcomes, not infrastructure to maintain
