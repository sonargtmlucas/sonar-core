# Pricing Logic

## Core principle
Price the system, not the agent.
Clients should not think about tokens, debugging, infrastructure, or agent count.
They buy a working workflow with a defined scope, clear controls, and ongoing improvement.

## The three tiers

### Architecture Sprint — $7,500 to $15,000 / 2–4 weeks
The entry point. Maps the client's workflow before any building happens.
Deliverables: ICP and signal matrix, data source audit, play logic definition, message inputs, scoring model, launch roadmap.
Why buy this first: no client should pay for deployment without a clear map. This is the intelligence sprint that makes deployment 3x faster.

### System Deployment — $15,000 to $50,000+ / setup fee
Connects everything and makes it run.
Deliverables: tools connected (CRM, outbound, enrichment), agents configured, scoring live, activation layer running, reporting dashboard.
Range width: $15K for a lean existing-stack connection. $50K+ for complex multi-system builds with custom agents.

### Managed Operation — $4,000 to $10,000/month (scope-based)
The recurring revenue layer. The system runs, we monitor, maintain, optimize, and improve.
Included: monitoring and maintenance, human approvals where needed, security and access controls, CRM and workflow integration, continuous optimization.
NOT included: major new workflows (separately scoped) or new system deployments.

## Pricing methodology
Value-based pricing. Not time, not tokens — results.
Rule of thumb: client should perceive 10x return on the first year investment.
Calculation example: system saves $38K/year in labor → charge 15% of that = ~$5,500 setup.
Maintenance retainer: 10–25% of original project cost per month.

## What clients buy
Not tools. Not agents. Not token math.
A team that doesn't sleep, doesn't quit, and gets better over time.
"You're not buying software. You're buying a pipeline operation run by AI with humans where it matters."

## Pricing floor
Both founders must approve:
- Any engagement below $7,500
- Performance-based components (% of pipeline, etc.)
- Equity-instead-of-cash arrangements
- Unusual scope or risk

## What we do not price
- Public fixed pricing for every possible engagement
- Per-seat or per-user pricing (we're not a SaaS tool)
- Monthly billing for one-time deliverables
- "Results guaranteed" pricing (we control the system, not the close rate)
